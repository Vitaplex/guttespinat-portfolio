## Ingredients
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_ingredients.jpg)

These are ingredients. They fit for two big portions (or one if you're a big guy like me):
* 2,5 chicken breasts (around 500-750 grams)
* 1x Low fat Crème Fraîche (300g)
* 2-3x garlic cloves
  * (Optional) Garlic powder
* 1x 250g Tortelloni (Any kind is good)
* Half a broccoli (including the stem)
* Some butter (oil is also good)

## Steps

### Step 1: Butter in the pan
Put butter (dairy butter or margarine) or any oils in the pan on medium heat. For my stovetop with digits 0-9, 6 is the sweet spot
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step1.jpg)

### Step 2: Water in the pot
Fill a pot with water and put the stove on medium heat. This will be for boiling the Tortellonis in.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step2.jpg)

### Step 3: Cut up the chicken
While the pan is warming up the butter, cut up however much chicken you have into small squares. Include tendons and everything (mmm...collagen)
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step3.jpg)

### Step 4: Cut up the broccoli
You can also use the stem, but only keep the core of the stem. The exterior is the most fiberous, and needs more cooking, thus its not as edible for this meal since it's pretty fast to cook.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step4.jpg)

### Step 5: SMASH garlic cloves for ***maximum*** aroma
Smash the garlic cloves and cut them up into small pieces
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step5.gif)

### Step 6: Put chicken in the pan
The butter should now be nice and browned, throw in the chicken meat.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step6.jpg)

### Step 7: Chicken is cooking
If you want to be effective, stirring the chicken pieces around to make them evenly cooked.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step7.jpg)

### Step 8: Put the broccoli in the pan
Put the broccoli in the pan and distribute them evenly, so they are also gettings baked. If the heat is high they will get a nice crust.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step8.jpg)

### Step 9: Put the Crème Fraîche in the pan
Put the Crème Fraîche in the pan, stirring it so it spreads evenly. It can be quite thick if it comes straight from the fridge, so stir it around so it becomes more liquid.  
This is also where I add the seasoning, like the garlic powder, salt and pepper.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step9.jpg)

### Step 10: Put the tortellonis in the pot
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step10.jpg)

### Step 11: Stir and wait for 5 minutes
Occasionally stir the chicken-broccoli mix and the tortellonis. I stir them with the lid of the pot.   
Wait for about 4-5 minutes, to let the tortellonis boil and the Crème Fraîche to settle.
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step11.jpg)

### Step 12: Transfer the tortelloni over into the pan
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step12.jpg)

### Step 13: Done! Enjoy!
![](/static/media/images/foodstuff/fw_step13.jpg)

## Macros and alternative ingredients
Chicken meat is rich in protein (around 23-30 grams/100g), and has one of the best kinds of protein, containing all the different amino acids nescesarry for the humany body. It contains practically no carbohydrates, and what little fat it contains is unsaturated, healthy fatty acids.  
Tortelloni is another source of protein (9-12g/100g), and good, complex carbohydrates - giving you a initial energy boost.

### Other options
This meal is also easily configurable if you dont have tortelloni or want something different: 
* Dont have tortellonis, just use other pasta instead.
  * Also, if you're feeling exceptionally lazy, and have space in the pan, just boil the tortellonis straight in the pan with everything else.
* If you want it more eastern/spicy; switch out the garlic spices for curry spices, and boil rice instead of tortellonis - a completely different meal with minimal changes
* Want more flavoring, add parmesan cheese and/or pesto to the chicken-broccoli stir
