### Funkis - Mid Century Modern house
**Release date:** *Dec. 2024*  
**Development time:** *Aug. 2024 - Dec. 2024*  
### [**View on the Steam workshop ‚Üó**](https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3380651069) 

Funkis (gm_funkis) is a sandbox map for Garry's Mod. I wanted to create a contemporary Mid Century Modern (MCM) - style home. The design and layout is original, but draws alot of inspiration from Norwegian "funkis"-architecture from the '60-'70s.
As an additional feature, I added an underground secret laboratory with a secret elevator entrance, to make the map more playful. The map was very well received and a night time version was also released.  
The map features lots of custom assets, as I wanted to challenge myself to get good at Blender. I made many custom assets which can be shown further down on this page.

### Polaria Systems - Underground Dungeon
**Release date:** *Jun. 2024*  
**Development time:** Mar. 2023 - Jun. 2024  
### [**View on NexusMods ‚Üó**](https://www.nexusmods.com/fallout4/mods/84454) 

A enormous underground facility dungeon for Fallout 4, structured as a simple loot-and-shoot adventure. The map features numerous areas to explore, secrets to find, mini-bosses, collectibles and of course lots of enemies to defeat.  

The dungeon is near DLC-sized and provide hours of exploration. A normal playthrough can take from 2 to 5 hours or more to complete. Spread throughout are also two unlockable checkpoints, so the player can traverse Polaria Systems at their own pace, and take breaks to head back to the Commonwealth.  
Polaria Systems also does not use any custom content apart from scripts - which makes the mod available for release on both PC, Xbox and Playstation!

The mod has been extremely well received and has been included in user-created essential modpacks for the game.

### Companion areas
An additional feature of the dungeon is "companion-specific" areas. These areas are areas with additional loot for the player, but requires that they bring a dog companion or any companion which can fit through small gaps, in order to press a button and unlock a door for the player to collect the loot. This was inspired from Lego-games, where certain characters are needed to unlock secrets or areas.  
This was a stupidly simple mechanic, but many people enjoyed it alot.

## Work Stuff
I work as a backend developer/devops engineer at [Norsk helsenett ‚Üó](https://www.nhn.no/), specializing in the development and operation of national e-health services.

### Work projects
[**üåê XcaDocumentSource - XCA Responding Gateway and Integrated Registry and Repository ‚Üó**](https://github.com/timmo-nhn/XcaDocumentSource)  
This is a web-service solution developed in [ASP.NET Core ‚Üó](https://dotnet.microsoft.com/en-us/apps/aspnet), facilitating the storing and retreiving of patient-healthcare-related information in a content-agnostic format.  
The solution implements this utilizing standards like [OASIS ebXML RegRep ‚Üó](https://docs.oasis-open.org/regrep/regrep-core/v4.0/os/regrep-core-rim-v4.0-os.pdf), **IHE** [XDS ‚Üó](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/TF/Volume1/ch-10.html) and [XCA ‚Üó](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/TF/Volume1/ch-18.html) and [XACML 2.0 ‚Üó](https://docs.oasis-open.org/xacml/2.0/access_control-xacml-2.0-core-spec-os.pdf) for access control.   
This allows **XcaDocumentSource** to act as a **document source** for hospitals and healthcare businesses who want to share their patients healthcare records, contributing to better communication and patient care.
